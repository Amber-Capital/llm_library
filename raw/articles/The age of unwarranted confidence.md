My perspective on macro research and trading strategy today.  
  
Tl;dr More than any time in my, 16 years focusing exclusively on macro, 40 year career, and, following of all financial history, those who assess macro conditions and investment implications are overly and undeservedly certain of their view.  
  
What do I mean?  
  
To have a high conviction about the future one must

1. Have confidence in the data that underlies that conviction.
2. Have confidence that the framework that one's using is correct.
3. Be capable of predicting policymaker actions and the consequences of those actions.
4. Be capable of predicting the implications of private sector innovations on the economy.

When I read this list and compare my confidences and capabilities relative to my historical capabilities and confidences I rank myself at an all time low in confidence in my view.  
There are two possibilities for why I particularly rank myself low at the moment. A. My skills needed to have confidence have degraded (I've lost my alpha) or B. The environment described in 1-4 is in fact one which low confidence is the correct posture.

Acknowledging A. As a possibility and focusing on B. Let me go through the list.  
  
**Data reliability.**

I've been trying to explain my view on economic data for a few months now but not sure I have done it well. Let me explain my view

Key U.S. hard economic data is less reliable than any time in my personal history due to the shutdown and legitimate attempts by the administration to improve and lower the expense of how data is collected and reported. I am not a conspiracy theory guy who thinks the data is intentionally being manipulated by the administration. I always place very low odds on that and would only say the extremely low odds of manipulation have gone up by a tiny amount. The data itself is more volatile and harder to interpret due to various bullwhip effects and other technical quirks such as the gold imports and exports impact but a litany of other things. I personally "corrected" the GDPNow estimate in Q1 2025 for gold imports. Many capable folks are doing this today with current data quirks. This effort is great stuff and worth following but misses the big point that hard data is simply less reliable. Survey (soft) data has always been low confidence for me and reflective of sentiment more than anything but today this data is also more volatile due to the increased political polarization of the respondents and the circularity of less reliable and volatile hard data influencing sentiment

**Confidence in Framework  
**For 6 years I have told everyone who listened that the framework of the past 40 years is no longer valid. The private sector business cycle simply isn't driving the economy and hasn't since Covid. This "framework" shift has provided me with great success having observed it at the many generational buy lows in stocks and highs in bonds (lows in yields) and the mostly resolute overweight of gold I have maintained. BUT having confidence in the "new framework" is simply ridiculous as it is a rare outcome with virtually no prior history to study and validate. Yet folks who were late to the shift are now all in and confident that they know how it works.

**Policymaker confidence.**

The current administration is activist that's happened before but almost certainly to less extreme by a large margin to my personal experience and the only proxy is Nixon and the exit from bretton woods and the gold standard. BUT this high policymaker activism is also highly volatile from month to month. Confidence in the actions of the Fed going forward HAS to be lower than any time in my history. Meaning how can one have high confidence in how the Fed will behave vs prior periods? Of course the courts are even involved in momentous decisions which I have never seen in my history and their decision is unpredictable.

**Private sector innovation.**  
Every few decades a technological innovation creates opportunity and uncertainty. Literally no one even the most informed on the base technology should have high confidence in the implications of AI on the future.

**Summary**  
All of these 4 things auger for a decreased confidence. But then place it in context of an incredibly big and leveraged economy which is both being dampened by policymakers and whose "spring constant" is almost definitely at a low "most springy" and strong confident views are incredibly dangerous in any direction  
  
**Nonetheless**  
It is my observation that many perhaps most macro researchers and strategists are more confident than ever in all aspects of their assessment. People who I really respect have wildly divergent views and produce content that is highly assertive despite no justification for such confidence. One of my favorites is certain the economy is much worse than people realize. Another favorite is certain that financial conditions are all that is important. Those with political bias have never been more biased and the provider have polarized and overconfident views and express them assertively. Lastly an industry of content providers deliver weedy details of data releases which while insightful create a bias that if you're smart/clever you can "read" the data. Instead of stepping back and saying the data is less reliable and ones cleverness many not provide any edge at all.  
  
My posture is simple. Whether I’ve lost the alpha or not, is yet to be determined, but, I am certain that I have the lowest level of certainty about the future as I have had in decades. Practically that informs my positioning. More gold and diversified fiat denominated cash and less long term assets than normal in my beta portfolio. Lower alpha risk than normal. Only take big swings at "generational prices".  
  
Macro will have its day in the sun again and is always valuable even today when adjusted for confidence and free of political bias.  
  
My own consumption of macro analysis is simple. I am aggressively pruning over confident assertive views and those impacted by obvious political bias. I suggest you do the same.
